Understanding Ohm's Law

What is Ohm's Law?

Ohm's Law is a fundamental principle in electronics and electrical engineering. It states that the current (I) passing through a conductor between two points is directly proportional to the voltage (V) across the two points and inversely proportional to the resistance (R) of the conductor.

The formula is: V = I × R

Where:
V = Voltage (Volts)
I = Current (Amperes)
R = Resistance (Ohms)
